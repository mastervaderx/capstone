Background Info

The war on drugs was not simply a war on drugs but a war on black americans. The government had used drugs as an excuse to arrest and imprison black americans. In doing so millions of Black Americans were abused and treated unfairly by the government’s Justice system. Just because of the color of their skin; black americans were given harsh and long sentences for crimes many didn’t even commit. Through the 13th amendment, black americans who were imprisoned became legal slaves. The effects of the War on Drugs still lives on in today’s justice system. People of color have been constantly targeted against and mistreated by the government. It is because we have been constantly oppressed by the same government that exclaims freedom; yet denies freedom to those who are not white; that we have become blind, unable to see the truth. The victims, whom are mostly African Americans, are locked up for years for crimes that white people can easily walk away from. This is clearly supported by the data I have found. The Herald Tribune collected data from millions of cases that determined the sentence gap between black americans and white americans for the same crime. This is due to judges racial bias when sentencing people of color. The Sentencing Project determined that people of color are given higher sentences than white people for the same crimes committed. This inequality is due to the roots of racism that has carved itself onto our society from long ago. Influenced by this racism, this racial bias; judges give people of color heavy prison sentences that many do not deserve and should not, cannot serve. Which is why we must try to stand up and realize our shackles placed on many if not all of us by birth. If we do nothing but allow ourselves to be treated this way then we will always be oppressed. If we desire to do nothing because we believe we are not affected by this then we will eventually face the repercussions. Alone, my voice is unheard, but if people come together our voices will be heard.

Our Plan

We'll review cases where judges provide unfair sentences and will try to appeal the sentence.

We'll take our review process to the government and they'll hopefully set minimum and maximum sentencing served based on each crime.

If the maximum for any certain crime is exceeded, then the expected outcome is for the case to be reopened.

A new judge will be in charge of the trail, but will have no demographic information of the person, hopefully providing fair sentencing.

If the same sentence is provided, the original sentence will remain. However, if the original judge is proven to have provided the wrong ruling, they'll receive a fine.

If judges continue providing many unfair sentences, then they'll be impeached.
